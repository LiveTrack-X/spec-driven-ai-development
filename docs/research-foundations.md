# Research Foundations

Status: Active reference, outside startup context
Scope: Source-to-decision record for SDAD 3.1.0 cross-model guidance

Sources inform bounded design decisions; they do not establish SDAD
effectiveness. Mixed productivity results are not consensus. No reported
percentage or benchmark score is an SDAD effectiveness claim. The principles
below are paraphrased, and vendor-specific interfaces transfer only where the
SDAD decision and limitation say they do.

| [SWE-bench](https://openreview.net/forum?id=VTF8yNQM66) | 2026-07-10 | Repository issue resolution can be evaluated against executable tests in realistic codebases. | Use task-shaped packets and deterministic regression checks where available. | A benchmark score does not transfer to a project, model generation, or general productivity claim. | Guidance |
| [SWE-agent](https://papers.nips.cc/paper_files/paper/2024/hash/5a7c947568c1b1328ccc5230172e1e7c-Abstract-Conference.html) | 2026-07-10 | The agent-computer interface and feedback from actions materially shape software-task performance. | Make tool outcomes observable and update the plan from environment evidence. | One interface and benchmark setting does not establish a universal agent architecture. | Guidance |
| [ReAct](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.03629) | 2026-07-10 | Alternating task-directed action with observations can ground subsequent decisions. | Record inspect-act-observe-update state without requesting or persisting hidden reasoning. | Research prompts and benchmark results do not require chain-of-thought capture in SDAD. | Guidance |
| [Reflexion](https://papers.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2023/hash/1b44b878bb782e6954cd888628510e90-Abstract-Conference.html) | 2026-07-10 | Feedback from failed attempts can improve later attempts when retained in a bounded useful form. | Retry only with new evidence or a changed hypothesis, and save concise durable decisions rather than transcripts. | Self-reflection text is not proof, and unbounded memory can add noise or sensitive data. | Guidance |
| [Agentless](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.01489) | 2026-07-10 | A simpler localization, repair, and validation pipeline can compete with more complex agent designs on some tasks. | Prefer hierarchical localization and the simplest workflow that meets the evidence contract. | Results are benchmark- and implementation-specific and do not rule out agents for other tasks. | Guidance |
| [Lost in the Middle](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.03172) | 2026-07-10 | More context does not guarantee effective use of information, especially when relevant material is poorly positioned. | Keep startup context bounded and load exact relevant slices progressively. | The studied retrieval behavior does not set a universal file-size or token threshold. | Guidance |
| [METR experienced-developer RCT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.09089) | 2026-07-10 | In one randomized setting, experienced developers' measured task time and perceived speed diverged. | Measure end-to-end outcomes in the actual repository and separate perception from observed evidence. | The sampled developers, repositories, and early-2025 tools do not support a universal negative effect. | Guidance |
| [Three code-completion field experiments](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/abs/10.1287/mnsc.2025.00535) | 2026-07-10 | Controlled field settings can show productivity benefits for particular code-completion tasks and populations. | Treat productivity as an empirical local question with a defined baseline and quality bar. | Completion-tool results do not transfer automatically to autonomous agents, all developers, or quality outcomes. | Guidance |
| [Observational agent-use productivity study](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5713646) | 2026-07-10 | Observed agent use can correlate with more merged changes in real development activity. | Track review burden and quality alongside throughput when evaluating an agent workflow. | Observational association does not establish causation or equivalent change quality. | Guidance |
| [SWE-bench Live](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.23419) | 2026-07-10 | Continuously refreshed tasks can reduce stale benchmark exposure and better test current capability. | Use held-out or fresh tasks for public comparative claims and record the evaluation date. | Freshness reduces but cannot eliminate leakage, verifier defects, or task-selection bias. | Guidance |
| [OpenAI SWE-bench Verified audit](https://openai.com/index/why-we-no-longer-evaluate-swe-bench-verified/) | 2026-07-10 | Benchmark tests and labels can contain defects that make aggregate scores misleading. | Audit deterministic checks and include semantic outcome review before relying on evaluation results. | One benchmark audit does not invalidate all benchmarks or substitute for project-specific validation. | Guidance |

## Use Boundary

Consult this matrix when a source-backed control changes or when designing an
evaluation. Do not load it during routine startup. Refresh a row's verification
date only after checking the source and re-evaluating both the adopted decision
and its limitation.
